Instructions for Picking up Food Donations

Food Safety Guidelines

  1. Contact the Donor: Reach out to the donor via phone or text message to confirm the details of the donation, including the pickup location, preferred pickup time, and any specific instructions.
  2. Prepare Necessary Supplies: Ensure you have all the necessary supplies for safe and hygienic food handling, including gloves, masks, hand sanitizer, and reusable bags or containers for transporting the food.
  3. Check Food Safety: Before accepting the donation, inspect the food items to ensure they are safe for consumption. Check for any signs of spoilage, damage to packaging, or expiration dates.
  4. Maintain Hygiene: Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before and after handling the food. If soap and water are not available, use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.
  5. Use Proper Packaging: Pack the donated food items securely in clean and sanitized containers or bags to prevent contamination during transport.
  6. Handle with Care: Handle the food items with care to avoid damage or spillage. Use caution when loading and unloading the items to prevent accidents or breakage.
  7. Transport Safely: Transport the donated food items to the designated drop-off location or distribution center using safe and sanitary transportation methods. Avoid overcrowding and practice social distancing during transit.
  8. Follow Instructions: Follow any specific instructions provided by the donor regarding handling or storing the food items. Respect the donor's preferences and ensure the food is handled according to their wishes.
  9. Document the Donation: Keep records of the donated food items, including the type and quantity of items collected, as well as any additional notes or instructions provided by the donor.
  10. Express Gratitude: Thank the donor for their generosity and support in helping to provide food for those in need. Acknowledge their contribution and express appreciation for their donation.
